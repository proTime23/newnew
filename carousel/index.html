Dragon-Ball

Dragon Ball (Japanese: ド ラ ゴ ン ボ ー ー ル, Hebrew: Dragon Ball) is a popular Japanese anime and manga series created by Akira Toriyama inspired by the popular Chinese novel "Journey to the West". The television series aired in Japan from 1986-1989. The manga series was published in "Weekly Shōnen Jump" (Ramadi Edition) in the years 1984-1995. 519 episodes of the series were bound in 42 volumes. The success of the series led to the production of four animated series in the Dragon Ball franchise of Tui Animation Studios, as well as the creation of special TV shows, video games, anime films (most of which were dubbed into Hebrew "Dragon Ball" and "Dragon Ball Z"), and even a live-movie American Action (2009) called "Dragon Ball: Evolution". The series has been appreciated by manga artists, and some have noted that it has inspired them to develop successful manga series, such as Masashi Kishimoto's Naruto and Aichiro Ode's One Piece. In Israel, unlike the other series in the franchise, the original series was not broadcast on television.
